Program Overview

Advance your expertise in holistic health and integrative medicine.

Building upon foundational knowledge, the Master's Degree of Allied Health delves deep into advanced research, leadership, and integrative health practices. This program empowers students to specialize in holistic health modalities while honing their abilities to lead in diverse healthcare environments.

Engage in rigorous inquiry into cutting-edge topics such as the gut biome, fascia functionality, and neuroplasticity. Elevate your understanding of both Eastern and Western health philosophies and prepare for leadership roles through hands-on internships and transformative research projects.

Advanced Research

Uncover the complexities of chronic disease and pain management through in-depth study of the gut microbiome and fascia.

Integrative Leadership

Develop the strategic and ethical leadership skills necessary for managing integrative healthcare teams.

Core Modules & Focus Areas

Specialized coursework designed for advanced practice and leadership

Advanced Health Sciences

  • Advanced Gut Microbiome Research and Its Impact on Chronic Disease
  • Exploring the Role of Fascia and Interstitium in Pain Management and Healing
  • Neuroplasticity and the Mind-Body Connection

Leadership and Global Health

  • Managing Integrative Healthcare Teams and Clinics
  • Healthcare Policy: Addressing Barriers in Eastern and Western Systems
  • Global Health Initiatives: Implementing Holistic Modalities

Specialized Modalities

  • Advanced Ayurvedic Practices and Diagnostics
  • Therapeutic Yoga for Chronic Conditions
  • Shinrin-yoku and Nature Therapy for Mental Health Practitioners

Research and Innovation

  • Conducting Clinical Studies in Holistic and Integrative Health
  • Exploring the Role of Technology in Supporting Allied Health Practices

Learning Outcomes

Graduate prepared for leadership and innovative practice in allied health

  • Develop advanced expertise in holistic health sciences and specialized modalities.
  • Gain leadership skills to manage and innovate within integrative healthcare systems.
  • Design and conduct original research in areas such as the gut biome, fascia, or Ayurvedic medicine.
  • Work collaboratively with Allied Care Inc. to integrate clinical and strategic health practices.

Frequently Asked Questions

Your questions about the Master's in Allied Health program answered.

What are the admission requirements?

Applicants are required to hold a relevant bachelor's degree and demonstrate a commitment to holistic and integrative health practices.

What career paths can you pursue?

Graduates can take up leadership roles in hospitals, integrative clinics, research institutes, and wellness centers globally.

How is the program delivered?

The program combines on-campus seminars, online interactive sessions, and hands-on clinical research projects.
